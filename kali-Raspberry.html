How to Install Kali Linux on Raspberry Pi

Kali linux

Kali Linux is a popular distribution for security and penetration testing, with hundreds of tools for ethical hacking, forensics, reverse engineering and more. In this blog post, we will show you how to install Kali Linux on a Raspberry Pi, a low-cost and versatile single-board computer that can run Kali Linux as well as other operating systems.

Prerequisites

What You Need To install Kali Linux on a Raspberry Pi, you will need the following:
- A Raspberry Pi 4 (or 3) with at least 2 GB of RAM. We recommend the 4 GB or 8 GB model for better performance.
- A microSD card with at least 16 GB of storage capacity. Class 10 cards are highly recommended for faster read and write speeds.
- A power supply for your Raspberry Pi, preferably with a USB-C connector for the Pi 4.
- A monitor, a keyboard and a mouse for your Raspberry Pi, or a way to access it remotely via SSH or VNC.
- A computer with an SD card reader and an internet connection to download and flash the Kali Linux image.

How to install

Download and Flash the Kali Linux Image The first step is to download the Kali Linux image for your Raspberry Pi from the official website. There are two versions available: one for 32-bit ARM architecture and one for 64-bit ARM architecture. The 32-bit version is compatible with all Raspberry Pi models, while the 64-bit version is only compatible with the Pi 3 and 4. The 64-bit version may offer some advantages in terms of performance and compatibility with some tools, but it may also have some issues with drivers and stability. We recommend using the 32-bit version unless you have a specific reason to use the 64-bit one. To download the image, go to https://www.kali.org/get-kali/#kali-arm and scroll down to the "RaspberryPi Foundation" section. Click on the image name or the torrent link to start the download. The image file is compressed in XZ format, so you will need a software that can extract it, such as 7-Zip or WinRAR. Once you have downloaded and extracted the image file, you will need to flash it to your microSD card using a software such as Balena Etcher or Raspberry Pi Imager. Both are free and easy to use, and they can automatically detect your SD card and verify the flashing process. You can download them from https://www.balena.io/etcher/ or https://www.raspberrypi.org/software/, respectively.

Flashing the image

Etcher
To flash the image with Etcher, follow these steps: - Open Etcher and click on "Select Image". Browse to the folder where you extracted the Kali Linux image file and select it. - Click on "Select Target" and choose your microSD card from the list. Make sure you select the correct device, as this process will erase all data on it. - Click on "Flash!" and wait for the process to complete. It may take several minutes depending on your SD card speed and size. - Once done, eject your microSD card safely from your computer.
Raspberry Pi Imager
To flash the image with Raspberry Pi Imager, follow these steps: - Open Raspberry Pi Imager and click on "Choose OS". Scroll down to "Use custom" and select your Kali Linux image file from your folder. - Click on "Choose SD Card" and select your microSD card from the list. Make sure you select the correct device, as this process will erase all data on it. - Click on "Write" and confirm by clicking on "Yes". Wait for the process to complete. It may take several minutes depending on your SD card speed and size. - Once done, eject your microSD card safely from your computer.

Boot the Device with kali

Boot Up Your Raspberry Pi with Kali Linux Now that you have flashed your microSD card with Kali Linux, you can insert it into your Raspberry Pi and boot it up. Connect your power supply, monitor, keyboard and mouse to your Raspberry Pi, or set up SSH or VNC access if you prefer. Turn on your Raspberry Pi and wait for it to load. The default username and password for Kali Linux are kali / kali. You should change them as soon as possible for security reasons. To do so, open a terminal window and type: sudo passwd root Enter a new password twice and confirm. Then type: sudo passwd kali Enter a new password twice and confirm.
