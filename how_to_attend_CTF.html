How To Attend a CTF Competition

Published 4 months ago5 min read4 comments

Capture the Flag (CTF) is a special kind of information security competition where participants are challenged to solve a series of problems in a limited amount of time. The problems are usually related to computer security, but can also include other topics such as cryptography, reverse engineering, programming, and forensics.

How To Attend a CTF Competition
If you are interested in cybersecurity, hacking or solving puzzles, you might want to try attending a CTF competition. CTF stands for Capture The Flag, and it is a special kind of cybersecurity competition designed to challenge its participants to solve computer security problems and/or capture and defend computer systems. In this blog post, I will give you some tips on how to attend a CTF competition and have fun.

What is a CTF competition?

A CTF competition is usually a team-based event where teams or individuals have to solve a number of challenges ranging from a scavenger hunt on wikipedia to basic programming exercises, to hacking your way into a server to steal data . Each challenge has a hidden flag that is proof of completion. The flag can be anything: a string of text, an image, a file, etc. The goal is to find as many flags as possible in the given time limit.
There are different types of CTF competitions:

- Jeopardy-style: The challenges are divided into categories (such as web, crypto, forensics, etc.) and each category has different levels of difficulty. The teams can choose which challenges they want to solve and earn points based on the difficulty.
- Attack-defense: The teams have their own network or server that they have to defend from other teams' attacks while also trying to attack other teams' systems. The teams earn points by capturing flags from other teams or by keeping their own system secure.
- Mixed: A combination of jeopardy-style and attack-defense.


How to find a CTF competition?

There are many online platforms that host CTF competitions regularly. Some examples are:
- [picoCTF](https://www.picoctf.org/): A beginner-friendly CTF platform created by Carnegie Mellon University. It has an ongoing game mode where you can practice your skills anytime and also hosts annual competitions with prizes for students.
- [Google CTF](https://capturetheflag.withgoogle.com/): A prestigious CTF platform hosted by Google. It has two rounds: a qualification round where anyone can participate and a final round where only the top teams are invited.
- [Hack The Box](https://www.hackthebox.com/hacker/ctf): A popular platform that offers both free and paid access to various hacking labs and challenges. It also hosts monthly themed CTF events with prizes.
You can also check out websites like [CTFtime](https://ctftime.org/) or [WeChall](https://www.wechall.net/) that list upcoming and past CTF competitions from various organizers.


How to prepare for a CTF competition?

Before attending a CTF competition, you should do some research on what kind of challenges you might encounter and what tools you might need. You can also practice your skills by solving previous challenges from past competitions or online platforms.
Some useful resources are:
- [CTF Field Guide](https://trailofbits.github.io/ctf/): A comprehensive guide on how to play CTFs covering topics such as cryptography, web exploitation, reverse engineering, binary exploitation and more.
- [Awesome CTF](https://github.com/apsdehal/awesome-ctf): A curated list of awesome resources related to CTFs such as write-ups, tools, books, courses and more. - [CTF Learn](https://ctflearn.com/): An online platform that offers beginner-friendly challenges with hints and solutions.
You should also form or join a team with people who share your interests and skills. Having teammates can help you learn new things, collaborate on challenges and have more fun.


How to enjoy a CTF competition?

During the competition, you should try your best but also remember to have fun. Here are some tips on how to enjoy yourself:
- Don't get frustrated if you can't solve a challenge right away. Sometimes it takes time and patience to figure out the solution. You can also ask for hints from the organizers or look for clues in the challenge description or files.
- Don't be afraid to try new things or learn new skills. You might discover something interesting or useful that you didn't know before.
- Don't cheat or break the rules. Cheating not only ruins the fun for yourself but also for others who play fairly. Follow the rules set by the organizers and respect their decisions.
- Don't forget about your health and well-being. Take breaks regularly, drink water, eat snacks and get enough sleep. You don't want to burn out or get sick
